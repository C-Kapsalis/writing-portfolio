Talk — "Source Control in Power BI"
===================================

A conference talk treated as a piece of documentation: a structured
explanation delivered to an audience, aimed at changing how they work.

**Venue:** Athens Fabric & Power BI Meetup, March 2025
**Materials:** *(add slides / recording link if available)*

The reader (audience)
---------------------

Business-intelligence developers who had never applied software-engineering
practices — version control, review, testing — to their data models, largely
because the tooling had never made it possible.

What it had to do
-----------------

Persuade a skeptical, practice-bound audience to adopt a genuinely new
workflow (representing Power BI models as text with TMDL, so they can be
diffed, reviewed and version-controlled). Persuasion, not just instruction —
which in Diátaxis terms makes it *explanation* with a call to action.

How I worked
------------

I built the talk the way I build documentation: a clear through-line, concrete
before/after, and claims anchored to sources (I cited the research on BI
project failure rates rather than asserting from opinion). I sequenced it to
move the audience from "this isn't my job" to "this is the missing basic," the
same arc a good explanation page walks a reader through.

Outcome
-------

The approach became standard practice on my team, and the talk circulated
beyond the room. It is the clearest example of documentation-as-advocacy in my
work — writing meant to move a community, not just inform it.

What I would improve
--------------------

I would pair the talk with a written how-to guide for the mechanical steps, so
the *inspiration* (explanation) and the *procedure* (how-to) live in their
proper, separate forms rather than being compressed into one session.
