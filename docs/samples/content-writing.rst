The reader
----------

Non-specialists — customers, newsletter subscribers, trade readers. The
opposite end of the spectrum from a reference page, and a deliberate inclusion:
technical writing for developer tools still has to reach people who do not
think like the tool's authors, and this shows I can meet a reader where they are.

How I worked
------------

I started from audience analysis — what the reader already knows and what they
came for — before choosing form and tone. On the performance side I ran A/B
tests and tracked the content against KPIs, so editorial decisions were checked
against real reader behaviour rather than taste alone. On a team I would work
with subject-matter contributors for accuracy and an editor for voice.

Why it is in a technical-writing portfolio
------------------------------------------

The instinct is identical to my docs work: identify the reader, serve their
actual need, measure whether it landed. Only the register changes.
